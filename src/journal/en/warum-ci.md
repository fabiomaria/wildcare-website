Contact Improvisation is the playful exploration of gravity, balance, and touch. Two people share a point of contact — a shoulder, a back, a hand — and let movement emerge from there. There are no fixed steps, no choreography, no pressure to perform. And above all: no roles. Nobody leads, nobody follows. Instead, a dialogue unfolds through the body.

That might sound abstract at first, but it's surprisingly concrete. In a typical session, you learn to give weight and receive weight. You feel how your balance shifts when another body touches yours. You notice where your boundaries lie — and practise communicating them. Not with words, but with a gesture, a change in muscle tone, a step to the side.

> In Contact, we learn something that applies everywhere: to be present, to listen, and to respond — not from the head, but from the body.

What happens goes far beyond dance. Body awareness, nervous system regulation, the ability to make boundaries tangible — these are skills just as relevant in everyday life as on the dance floor. Anyone who has practised paying attention to subtle signals in dance will also notice them in conversations, relationships, and conflicts. Contact Improvisation is not just a dance form. It is training for more conscious coexistence.
