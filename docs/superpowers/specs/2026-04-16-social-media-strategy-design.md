# Wild Care Social Media Strategy — 2-Week Sprint

**Date:** 2026-04-16
**Goal:** Fill Bewegungsrevolution registrations by May 11, 2026
**Owner:** Fabio
**Platforms:** Instagram (primary) + Facebook (reposts)
**Effort:** ~1.5h/week

---

## Context

Wild Care is starting from zero on social media. The immediate priority is driving registrations for Bewegungsrevolution — a free, funded 6-week Contact Improvisation block for total beginners, starting May 11, 2026.

---

## Strategy: Content Calendar Sprint

4 posts over 2 weeks, created in one sitting per week and posted/scheduled across both platforms.

---

## Week 1 — Awareness

### Post 1 (Day 1–2): The Announcement
- **Visual:** Best existing photo or short clip
- **Copy hook:** "6 Wochen. Kostenlos. Für alle."
- **CTA:** Link in bio → Tally registration form
- **Goal:** Reach and first impressions

### Post 2 (Day 4–5): What is Contact Improvisation?
- **Visual:** Short video clip from existing footage
- **Copy:** 2–3 sentences demystifying CI. Address the fear directly: "Keine Tanzerfahrung nötig. Wirklich keine."
- **CTA:** Link in bio → Jetzt anmelden
- **Goal:** Remove the biggest barrier (unfamiliarity)

---

## Week 2 — Social Proof + Urgency

### Post 3 (Day 8–9): Testimonial
- **Visual:** Designed quote graphic or clean text post
- **Content:** Use existing testimonials (Anna or Iris — already on website)
- **CTA:** Link in bio → Jetzt anmelden
- **Goal:** Trust and conversion

### Post 4 (Day 12–13): Last Call
- **Visual:** Countdown or bold typography
- **Copy:** Urgency framing — "Noch X Plätze frei" + date reminder
- **CTA:** Hard repeat of registration link
- **Goal:** Convert fence-sitters before May 11

---

## Copy Principles

- Warm, direct, no dance-world jargon
- Write like talking to a curious friend who has never danced
- Pull from existing website copy (already strong)
- Every post ends with one clear action: "Link in Bio → Jetzt anmelden"

---

## After the Sprint

Evaluate what worked (reach, clicks, registrations) and decide whether to continue with a lighter maintenance rhythm or run another sprint for the next event.
